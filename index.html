II · Continuous proof of storage

Not stored once.
Proven forever.

Every thirty seconds, every prover in the network re-verifies a fresh cryptographic challenge against your data. A missed proof is an immediate, on-chain economic event.

Storage providers stake against the durability of what they hold. Clients pay only for the time the data is provably retrievable.

This is not a backup. It is a living guarantee, renewed thousands of times an hour, recorded permanently to a verifiable ledger.

III · Architecture

Three roles.
One cryptographic contract.

Clients store. Provers prove. Ethereum verifies. No new chain. No bridges. No trust.
